## Audits should create decisions.

A useful audit does not merely describe everything a tool can detect. It explains what appears weak, why it may matter, what evidence supports the concern, how confident the conclusion is, what the fix would require, and what should happen first.

Technical warnings, weak service pages, poor review signals, missing analytics, provider-access problems, and unclear customer paths may all deserve attention. They are not automatically equal problems.

The work is to rank them intelligently.

## Common research and audit packages

### Focused Decision Review

A narrow review of one defined question, system, or customer path.

Examples:

- Why are qualified inquiries not reaching the business?
- Does the current website need repair or redesign?
- Which service page deserves attention first?
- Is the current provider’s visible work understandable and useful?
- Is measurement good enough to support a larger investment?

### Technical SEO Audit

A prioritized review of the technical conditions that affect whether important pages can be crawled, indexed, understood, used, measured, and maintained.

The review may include:

- Crawl access
- Indexation signals
- Sitemaps
- Canonical signals
- Status codes
- Redirects
- Duplicate and orphaned paths
- Internal linking
- Metadata and headings
- Structured information
- Performance and mobile behavior
- Migration risks
- Analytics and search-property access

### Local Market and Competitor Report

A study of the visible local market, including competitor websites, business profiles, reviews, service areas, result types, customer paths, website quality, and possible gaps.

The report should distinguish observed public facts from hypotheses. It should not pretend that public competitor data reveals private revenue, lead volume, conversion rates, or strategy.

### Analytics and Measurement Readiness Review

A review of what is collected, what is missing, whether important actions are defined, how sources disagree, which reports are useful, and what must be configured before reliable conclusions can be made.

When usable historical data does not exist, the deliverable is a Measurement Readiness Report rather than a fabricated baseline.

### Provider Performance or Second-Opinion Review

A review of supplied reports, visible website changes, access, ownership, deliverables, technical conditions, and whether the current provider’s work can be connected to a clear purpose.

Boho does not make legal conclusions about a provider contract or claim misconduct without evidence. The review is intended to clarify the work and the next business decision.

### Migration Risk Review

A review of domains, URLs, content, forms, analytics, search properties, DNS, email dependencies, integrations, access, and other conditions that could affect a redesign or platform move.

## The Boho report standard

Every substantial report should make the research process inspectable.

### Start with a data receipt

The report should identify:

- Reporting or review period
- Websites, properties, markets, or systems reviewed
- Sources used
- Last successful collection or review date
- Missing, stale, unavailable, or failed sources
- Relevant filters and exclusions
- Methodology version
- Analyst review date
- Material assumptions

### Separate three kinds of statements

**Observed** means directly supported by the reviewed evidence.

**Inferred** means a reasonable interpretation that still contains uncertainty.

**Recommended** means an action proposed on the basis of the evidence, business context, risk, cost, and stated assumptions.

These labels should not be collapsed into one paragraph of artificial confidence.

### Use a consistent finding format

A material finding should identify:

- The issue or signal
- The evidence
- Why it may matter
- Confidence and limitations
- Recommended action
- Priority
- Estimated effort or difficulty
- Responsible owner
- Validation signal
- Review date

### Apply quality gates

A report should disclose or stop when:

- A required source failed
- A source is materially stale
- Date ranges do not match
- Conversion definitions changed
- Totals disagree beyond an accepted threshold
- There is insufficient evidence for the requested conclusion
- A recommendation lacks a supporting reason

### Limit the main decision set

The main report should usually lead with three to five priority actions, one section identifying what should not be purchased yet, and a validation plan. Broader data can remain available in an appendix or underlying system.

## Free platform or paid report?

### Use the free platform when

- You want supported self-service data views.
- You are comfortable interpreting the information yourself.
- You do not need a recurring written recommendation.
- Your data sources are already configured well enough to use.

### Add a paid monthly report when

- Data sources disagree or require review.
- The business needs interpretation in market and operational context.
- Priorities must be limited to what is most useful now.
- A written record is needed for decisions, providers, or future comparison.
- The system is large enough that manual reconciliation or grouping is required.

### Commission a one-time audit when

- The decision is specific and does not require monthly repetition.
- A redesign, migration, provider change, market entry, technical repair, or larger investment is being considered.
- The business needs a documented roadmap before implementation.

## Frequently asked questions

### Is the initial review really free?

Yes. It uses public information to identify the likely next step. It is not a complete audit, private-account review, written strategy, provider recovery, or implementation project.

### Is the Boho Analytics Platform really free?

Yes, for supported uses and data sources. Custom setup, tracking repair, historic reconstruction, private integrations, complex ecommerce configuration, or unsupported data work may require a paid scope.

### What am I paying for in the monthly report?

You are paying for human data-quality review, source reconciliation, interpretation, prioritization, limitations, and a written decision record. You are not paying merely for access to the dashboard.

### Why does reporting cost more for ecommerce or large websites?

Checkout events, revenue, catalogs, many templates, multiple properties, cross-domain journeys, conflicting platforms, custom groupings, and damaged tracking require more collection review, reconciliation, interpretation, and risk control than one small website with a simple form path.

### Does a report include implementation?

No, unless the written scope says otherwise. Reporting-only plans provide analysis and recommendations. Website changes, SEO work, tracking repair, content, migration, and other implementation are separate.

### Do you guarantee that an audit will improve rankings or revenue?

No. An audit can identify evidence, risk, priorities, and recommended actions. Outcomes depend on implementation, market conditions, platforms, competitors, customers, time, and the business itself.

### What happens if the data is bad or missing?

The report should say so. Boho may provide a Measurement Readiness Report explaining what is missing, what should be configured, what can currently be concluded, and what cannot.

### Can you review another provider’s reports?

Yes, when the client is authorized to share them. Boho can evaluate whether the reports identify sources, work completed, evidence, limitations, priorities, and useful next actions. The review is not a legal opinion about the provider contract or conduct.

### Can a paid audit be credited toward implementation?

Yes, when it is an eligible one-time review and the qualifying larger engagement falls within the same category, concerns substantially the same system, is accepted within 90 days, and meets the published conditions.

### How many competitors do you review?

The number depends on the research question, market, data quality, and written scope. A useful sample is better than a large list assembled without a decision in mind.

### Can you analyze payment or ecommerce information?

Boho may review approved aggregate analytics, product, order, and conversion information where appropriate. Boho does not need or request card numbers, banking credentials, authentication secrets, or raw payment details for ordinary reporting.

### Will the report tell us what not to buy?

It should. A useful report identifies low-priority work, unsupported ideas, missing prerequisites, and spending that should wait until better evidence exists.
