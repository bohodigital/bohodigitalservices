## A monthly retainer should have a job.

A recurring plan makes sense when the work is genuinely recurring. Search results change. Competitors publish and improve. Services, locations, products, and customer questions evolve. Websites accumulate technical debt. New content needs internal links, measurement, maintenance, and follow-through.

That does not mean every business needs a retainer.

If one repair, audit, migration, or website project would solve the current problem more economically, Boho should recommend that instead. Ongoing work should continue because the next cycle has a useful purpose, not because cancellation has been made inconvenient.

## This service is a good fit when

- The website already has a usable technical and content foundation.
- The business operates in a competitive local, regional, professional, or ecommerce market.
- Important services, locations, categories, or customer questions need continued development.
- Search visibility, content quality, site health, and customer paths need to improve together.
- The business wants named priorities and documented work rather than a generic monthly task list.
- The business can provide timely access, information, review, and approval.
- The business can answer and qualify the additional inquiries it hopes to create.
- The business wants reporting connected to decisions, not merely a larger collection of charts.

## This service may not be the right first step when

- The website is broken, inaccessible, or controlled by a provider the business needs to leave.
- A full redesign or migration is required before ongoing work can be responsible.
- Valid measurement does not exist and a baseline or measurement-readiness project is needed first.
- One focused repair would solve the visible problem.
- Nobody can provide business information, review content, approve changes, or maintain accurate offers.
- The requested strategy depends on fake locations, fake reviews, spam links, mass low-value content, or guaranteed rankings.
- The only goal is “more traffic” without defining which services, products, locations, or customer actions matter.

## What ongoing SEO may include

The approved monthly scope may combine several kinds of work. Not every item is performed every month, and a narrow plan should not be presented as unlimited access to every service Boho offers.

### Technical SEO and site health

Boho may review and improve crawl access, indexation signals, XML sitemaps, canonical signals, status codes, redirects, internal links, page hierarchy, metadata, structured information, mobile behavior, performance, and other technical conditions that affect whether important pages can be found, understood, used, and maintained.

Technical work is prioritized by likely business impact and risk. An audit tool can produce hundreds of warnings. It cannot decide which warning deserves the next dollar.

### Local SEO and market relevance

For eligible local and service-area businesses, work may include business-profile alignment, accurate business information, service and location structure, review-process guidance, local content, internal linking, competitor observation, and improvements to the pages people use when choosing a provider.

Boho does not create fake offices, fake service areas, fake reviews, or thin location pages built only to capture city-name variations.

### Service, category, and commercial content

Boho may plan, edit, improve, or build pages around the services, products, categories, markets, and questions most closely connected to business value. The goal is not to publish the largest possible quantity of material. The goal is to make useful commercial information easier to find, understand, compare, and act on.

Depending on the written scope, content work may include briefs, page structures, editing, internal-link plans, title and description improvements, consolidation, updates, or new page development.

### Internal linking and information architecture

Search visibility and customer understanding both depend on how pages relate to one another. Boho may improve navigation, page hierarchy, contextual links, service and location relationships, category structure, orphaned-page access, and the path from educational material to relevant commercial pages.

### Customer paths and conversion clarity

More visits are not useful when the wrong people arrive or serious prospects cannot find the next step. Work may include service positioning, page structure, calls to action, forms, phone links, booking paths, qualification language, trust information, and confirmation behavior.

Boho does not guarantee conversion rates, leads, sales, or revenue. It can identify friction, improve the system, and measure supported signals.

### Analytics, reporting, and decision support

Ongoing work may use supported data from the Boho Analytics Platform and approved external sources. Reporting should identify sources, missing or stale information, relevant filters, observed signals, reasonable inferences, recommendations, limitations, and the next decision.

When usable historical data does not exist, Boho provides a measurement-readiness plan instead of inventing a baseline.

### AI and answer-system visibility

Where relevant, Boho may improve the same foundations that help search engines, answer systems, and people understand the business: clear entity information, useful page structure, direct answers, accurate service details, crawlable content, source-worthy material, internal relationships, and consistent public information.

Boho does not sell a secret shortcut for placement in AI-generated answers. There is no responsible way to guarantee inclusion, citation, ranking, or recommendation by a search engine, language model, answer engine, or third-party platform.

## Reporting-only or implementation?

### Analyst-reviewed monthly reporting

Reporting-only plans are for businesses that want regular interpretation without ongoing website changes.

A reporting-only plan may include:

- Supported dashboard access
- Data-quality review
- Source and collection status
- Observed, inferred, and recommended labels
- Three to five prioritized findings
- Limitations and confidence notes
- A written decision record
- A next-review date

Reporting-only plans do not include website changes, content production, technical repair, custom tracking implementation, hosting, managed email, or unlimited consultation unless the written scope expressly says otherwise.

**Starting price:** $95 per month

### Ongoing implementation retainers

Implementation retainers combine reporting with an agreed amount of recurring SEO, content, website, technical, local, conversion, or measurement work.

The written proposal should state:

- The websites, properties, markets, or locations covered
- The included monthly work capacity
- The kinds of work that may be performed
- Communication and meeting expectations
- Content responsibilities
- Hosting or email benefits, if any
- Third-party costs
- The billing schedule
- Any initial term
- Cancellation and notice requirements
- Work that remains outside the retainer

**Starting price:** $450 per month

## The monthly operating cycle

### 1. Review the current system

Boho reviews the approved website, search, content, technical, local, customer-path, and measurement information available for the cycle.

### 2. Name the priority

The current priority should be stated in business language. Examples include improving a high-value service path, correcting a technical loss, strengthening one local market, repairing measurement, or consolidating weak content.

### 3. Complete the approved work

Boho performs the work included in the monthly scope. Material changes in direction, cost, access, or risk require approval rather than appearing later as an unexplained surprise.

### 4. Verify what changed

The relevant pages, links, forms, signals, deployments, or records are checked. Search engines and third-party platforms may take additional time to recrawl, process, display, or react to changes.

### 5. Document the cycle

The monthly record explains what was completed, what was observed, what remains uncertain, what should happen next, and which possible tasks were deliberately deferred.

## When ongoing work should stop

Boho should recommend reducing, pausing, or ending a recurring engagement when:

- The agreed objectives are substantially complete.
- The remaining work no longer justifies the cost.
- A focused project would be more economical.
- The website needs a redesign, migration, or rescue before further SEO work is sensible.
- Useful measurement cannot be established.
- The client cannot provide required access, information, approval, or follow-through.
- The business cannot responsibly handle additional demand.
- The requested direction depends on deceptive, unsupported, or platform-prohibited tactics.

A retainer is not a membership fee for being allowed to ask what happened.

## Frequently asked questions

### Do you guarantee rankings, traffic, leads, or revenue?

No. Search engines, answer systems, competitors, markets, customers, platforms, and the client’s own operations affect outcomes. Boho can define the hypothesis, complete agreed work, document decisions, and measure supported signals. It cannot responsibly guarantee a particular ranking, traffic level, lead count, sale, or revenue result.

### How long does SEO take?

There is no universal timeline. Technical corrections may be deployed quickly, while crawling, indexing, ranking, market response, and customer behavior can take longer and remain uncertain. The written proposal should distinguish the work schedule from the time external systems may take to react.

### What happens every month?

Each cycle should name the current priority, complete the work included in the scope, verify relevant changes, document evidence and limitations, and recommend the next decision. The specific activity changes with the business need rather than repeating a ceremonial checklist.

### What is the difference between reporting-only and implementation?

Reporting-only provides analyst review, interpretation, priorities, and a written decision record. Implementation includes an agreed amount of actual website, SEO, content, local, technical, conversion, or measurement work.

### Do I need a redesign before starting SEO?

Not necessarily. A useful existing website may only need focused improvement. A redesign or migration should be recommended only when the current structure, platform, content, ownership, accessibility, performance, or customer path prevents responsible improvement.

### Is local SEO included?

It can be. The proposal should state whether business-profile work, local service pages, location structure, review-process guidance, local market analysis, and related implementation are included.

### Is content included?

Only when the written scope says so. Content work may range from editing and page planning to new page development. Routine long-form writing, photography, specialist claims, and large content programs should not be assumed inside the minimum retainer.

### Do you work on AI search visibility?

Boho may improve the underlying clarity, structure, crawlability, entity information, useful answers, and source quality that can help both people and automated systems understand a business. Boho does not guarantee placement, citation, or recommendation in AI-generated answers.

### Are hosting and email included?

They may be included with a qualifying implementation retainer when the proposal expressly says so. Reporting-only plans do not qualify. The proposal defines whether email means routing, aliases, transactional messages, stored mailboxes, or another service.

### Do you require a long contract?

The proposal states whether the engagement is month-to-month or includes an initial term, together with cancellation and notice requirements. Ongoing work should continue because it remains useful, not because leaving has been made deliberately painful.

### Can we start smaller?

Often, yes. Boho may recommend one market, one service group, one technical problem, one measurement repair, or one high-value customer path first. A smaller scope still needs to be complete enough to produce a responsible result.

### Who owns the accounts and work?

Boho generally prefers client ownership of durable business assets such as domains, analytics properties, search properties, business profiles, content, and major platform accounts. Exact ownership, licensing, access, and handoff terms are stated in the written engagement.
