## A business website has a job.

It should explain what the business does, who it helps, where it works, why someone should trust it, and what the visitor should do next.

It should also behave like a durable business asset. It should work on mobile devices, remain readable at increased zoom, support search discovery, load efficiently, send inquiries to the right place, preserve important records, and avoid trapping the business inside an arrangement nobody understands.

Visual quality matters. A distinctive website can communicate confidence before a visitor reads a paragraph. But design must support the business. A beautiful page that hides the service, buries the contact path, or collapses on a phone is still a failed tool.

## Choose the right level of work

### Focused Website Improvement

A focused improvement is appropriate when the current website has a sound enough foundation to keep.

The project may address one to three high-value problems, such as:

- An unclear homepage or service page
- A weak contact or booking path
- Mobile usability problems
- Accessibility barriers
- Performance issues
- Broken forms
- Confusing navigation
- Search metadata and crawl problems
- Missing trust or ownership information
- A high-value page that does not support the customer decision

**Starting price:** $750

### New Website

A new website is appropriate for a new business, a new offer, a major repositioning, or a current system that cannot reasonably be preserved.

The work may include business and audience definition, sitemap planning, page structure, content support, visual design, responsive implementation, forms, search foundations, analytics readiness, launch verification, and handoff.

**Starting price:** $1,500

### Substantial Website Redesign

A redesign is appropriate when the current website no longer supports the business because of its structure, content, visual system, platform, technical condition, customer path, ownership, or maintainability.

A redesign should preserve useful content, URLs, search value, forms, data, and brand assets where appropriate. “New” is not automatically better than “understood.”

**Starting price:** $1,500

### Rescue + Redesign

A Rescue + Redesign combines provider exit, ownership and access work, migration planning, preservation, and a replacement website.

This path is appropriate when the business needs to leave a provider and the existing website is too fragile, unclear, expensive, or technically unsound to preserve as the long-term system.

**Starting price:** Determined through a specific quote using the website and migration scopes.

## This service is a good fit when

- The website is unclear, dated, slow, inaccessible, or difficult to use on mobile devices.
- Important services, products, locations, or customer actions are buried.
- The site no longer represents the business accurately.
- The business needs a professional website but does not need an oversized custom application.

## This service may not be the right fit when

- The request depends on copying a competitor’s design or content.
- The business wants fabricated testimonials, projects, reviews, credentials, team members, or performance claims.

- The desired budget cannot support a complete, functional, responsible version of the requested system.
- Nobody is authorized to approve content, ownership, access, or launch decisions.
- The project requires a large custom application better handled as a separately scoped custom system.

## What Boho designs around

### The offer

A visitor should understand what the business sells, which services or products matter most, and what makes the offer relevant without excavating the navigation.

### The customer

The structure, language, proof, questions, and calls to action should match what the intended customer needs before acting.

### The market

Service, location, category, and educational pages should reflect the real market rather than a generic template or a pile of keyword variations.

### Trust

The website should use real process, ownership, policies, operator information, examples, reviews, credentials, guarantees, and results only where they are accurate and supportable.

### Customer action

Calls, forms, quote requests, booking paths, purchases, directions, and other valuable actions should be obvious, usable, and routed correctly.

### Ownership and operation

The business should understand the domain, hosting, accounts, files, content, deployment, analytics, forms, ongoing costs, and exit path.

## The website process

### 1. Review the current business and website

Boho reviews the public website, business model, services or products, market, customer actions, current provider, visible technical condition, and available content.

### 2. Decide whether to repair, redesign, or rebuild

The recommendation should identify the smallest complete scope reasonably capable of meaningful improvement. A full redesign should not be recommended merely because redesign work is easier to sell than careful repair.

### 3. Map the structure

Boho defines the sitemap, page roles, customer paths, content responsibilities, internal relationships, and the information required for approval.

### 4. Design and build

The visual system and page structures are developed around the approved business requirements. Work proceeds iteratively with client review at defined decision points.

### 5. Verify and launch

Boho checks the approved pages, mobile behavior, forms, primary actions, links, redirects where applicable, metadata, deployment, and other agreed launch conditions.

### 6. Document and hand off

The final record explains the affected systems, verified ownership, providers, accounts, completed work, relevant access guidance, recurring costs, maintenance needs, open risks, and recommended next steps.

Where usable analytics data exists, the handoff may include an initial baseline. Where it does not, Boho provides measurement-readiness guidance rather than fabricating history.

## Hosting, email, ownership, and exit

### Hosting

Eligible websites may be hosted at no separate Boho service charge while a qualifying implementation retainer remains active, current, and in good standing.

Project and reporting-only clients may purchase eligible standalone hosting or managed website operations separately.

### Email

A proposal may include a defined business email service. Depending on the scope, that may mean custom-domain routing, aliases, forwarding, transactional website messages, stored mailboxes, domain authentication, migration, filtering, or administration.

“Included email” does not automatically mean every email function. The proposal must state the provider, mailbox or routing scope, storage, sending limits, retention, support, third-party licenses, and exit treatment.

### Ownership

Boho generally prefers client ownership of durable business assets, including domains, major platform accounts, analytics properties, search properties, approved content, and project deliverables, subject to any disclosed platform or licensing limits.

### Exit

The final handoff should explain:

- What the client owns
- What Boho manages
- Which providers are involved
- Which recurring costs exist
- How the website is deployed
- How forms and analytics operate
- What maintenance is required
- What happens when hosting, maintenance, or the client relationship ends

No website, hosting platform, DNS provider, network, or email service is outage-proof. Any binding service level must be stated in the written engagement.

## Frequently asked questions

### Can you really build a website starting at $1,500?

Yes, when the project fits the stated minimum scope: a small public website, limited pages and templates, straightforward functionality, prepared business information, client-supplied or lightly edited content, and no major migration, ecommerce, or custom integration. Larger or more complicated projects receive a different quote.

### How do I know whether I need a redesign or a focused repair?

The free initial review is designed to help make that decision. Boho should recommend repair when the existing structure, platform, content, and ownership remain useful. A redesign is appropriate when preserving the current system would retain the same underlying problems or cost more than replacing it responsibly.

### Is copywriting included?

Only when the written scope says so. The minimum website assumes client-supplied or lightly edited content. Boho may separately scope page planning, editing, copy development, research, or content migration.

### Is SEO included in a website project?

A responsible website build should include basic search foundations such as crawlable structure, metadata fields, internal links, redirects when required, useful headings, and measurement readiness. Ongoing SEO, content development, market research, and ranking improvement are separate unless expressly included.

### Do you build ecommerce websites?

Boho may accept ecommerce or payment-enabled work after reviewing the catalog, checkout, tax, shipping, payment, account, transaction-email, data, security, platform, and support requirements. Ecommerce does not qualify for the $1,500 minimum.

### Which platform will you use?

The platform depends on the business, editing needs, functionality, ownership, performance, integrations, budget, and long-term operating requirements. The proposal should identify the platform and explain the reason for the recommendation.

### Will the website be accessible?

Boho designs and tests toward accessible use and the stated project standard, but no provider should casually claim universal or permanent compliance across every page, device, browser, assistive technology, third-party component, or future content change. Any formal certification or specialist legal review must be separately scoped.

### Is hosting included?

It may be included with a qualifying implementation retainer when the proposal expressly says so. Otherwise eligible hosting starts at the public standalone rate shown on the Pricing page.

### Is business email included?

A defined email service may be included when stated in the proposal. The proposal must distinguish routing, aliases, transactional messages, and stored mailboxes, together with provider costs and limits.

### Will I own the website?

Ownership and licensing depend on the platform, third-party components, and written agreement. Boho generally prefers client ownership of durable assets and documents any limits before work begins.

### How long will the project take?

The specific quote states the schedule. A small site with approved content and one decision-maker can move more quickly than a content-heavy, migration-heavy, ecommerce, or multi-stakeholder project. Client access, content, approvals, and provider cooperation affect timing.

### What happens after launch?

The client receives documentation and handoff. Ongoing SEO, hosting, managed website operations, content, and later improvements may continue under a separate approved scope. Clients who do not continue monthly may receive practical maintenance and DIY guidance appropriate to the project.
